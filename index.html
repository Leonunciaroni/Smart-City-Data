California Case: Urban Pollution Crisis

The map on the side uses NASA satellite data to diagnose a pollution crisis. In metropolises like Los Angeles and San Diego, heavy traffic generates large amounts of Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2).

This pollutant doesn't disperse easily due to geographical factors like thermal inversion. This climate phenomenon acts as a "lid" over the city, trapping NO2 and other gases.

This is the high-risk scenario: If you were the leader, what would be your strategy to solve this public health and urban crisis?

Solutions for Resilient Cities

Discover proven strategies that are transforming urban areas into healthier, sustainable and smarter environments.

🌳

Urban Green Corridors

📊 How It Works

Creation of continuous green axes connecting urban areas, parks and avenues with dense vegetation and natural shading systems.

  • Tree canopy coverage increases from 15% to 40%
  • Temperature reduction: 2-5°C in urban areas
  • NO₂ filtration: Up to 60% improvement

💚 Environmental Impact

Natural air purification through photosynthesis and evapotranspiration processes.

-45% Air Pollution
-3°C Urban Temperature
+80% Biodiversity

🏗️ Implementation

Strategic planting along transportation corridors and urban centers.

1 Identify key urban heat islands
2 Plant native tree species
3 Connect green spaces
4 Maintain and monitor growth
🚌

Sustainable and Electric Mobility

📊 How It Works

Gradual replacement of combustion fleets with electric buses, expansion of bike lanes, and implementation of low-emission zones.

  • Electric bus fleet: 100% conversion in 5 years
  • Bike lane network: +300% expansion
  • Low-emission zones: City center restrictions

💚 Environmental Impact

Drastic reduction in NO₂ emissions and urban noise pollution.

-60% NO₂ Emissions
-70% Noise Pollution
+45% Public Transport Use

🏗️ Implementation

Phased approach with infrastructure development.

1 Electric bus procurement
2 Charging infrastructure
3 Bike lane expansion
4 Zone implementation
🏙️

Green Roofs and Walls

📊 How It Works

Incentives for vertical gardens, green roofs, and recovery of urban rivers and canals to create natural cooling systems.

  • Green roof coverage: 25% of buildings
  • Vertical gardens: 50km of urban walls
  • River restoration: 15km of urban waterways

💚 Environmental Impact

Improved CO₂ absorption and creation of urban microclimates.

-35% Surface Temperature
+50% CO₂ Absorption
-40% Stormwater Runoff

🏗️ Implementation

Building codes and incentive programs.

1 Building code updates
2 Tax incentives
3 Technical support
4 Community programs
